Used Plastic Syringes

Material Composition

Plastic Biomedical Waste

Waste Category & Bin

Red Bin

Environmental Fact

Improper syringe disposal creates plastic pollution and spreads infection risk.

Safe Disposal Protocol

  • Separate needle using hub cutter.
  • Disinfect with 1% hypochlorite.
  • Place plastic body in red bin.
  • Send for recycling after sterilization.

Metallic Needles & Scalpels

Material Composition

Metal Sharps Waste

Waste Category & Bin

White Bin

Environmental Fact

Open disposal of sharps can transmit HIV and Hepatitis.

Safe Disposal Protocol

  • Do not recap needles.
  • Use puncture-proof sharp container.
  • Destroy needle using needle destroyer.
  • Autoclave before recycling.

Blood-Soaked Gauze / Cotton

Material Composition

Soiled Infectious Waste

Waste Category & Bin

Yellow Bin

Environmental Fact

Infected cotton can contaminate soil and groundwater.

Safe Disposal Protocol

  • Wear PPE while handling.
  • Place directly into yellow bag.
  • Do not compress manually.
  • Send for incineration.

Material Composition

Glass Waste

Blue Bin

Environmental Fact

Chemical residues may contaminate nearby ecosystems.

Safe Disposal Protocol

Expired Cytotoxic Drugs

Material Composition

Hazardous Pharmaceutical Waste

Waste Category & Bin

Yellow Bin

Environmental Fact

Cytotoxic drugs damage DNA of aquatic organisms.

Safe Disposal Protocol

  • Use PPE and double gloves.
  • Store in leak-proof container.
  • Label as cytotoxic waste.
  • Dispose by high-temperature incineration.
