    Expired Tablets

    Material Composition

    Pharmaceutical Waste (Active Chemical Compounds + Coating Materials)

    Waste Category & Bin

    Yellow Bin

    Hospital Department

    Pharmacy / Ward / Medicine Storage Unit

    Environmental Impact

    Improper disposal can contaminate groundwater and contribute to antimicrobial resistance in environmental microorganisms.

    Risk Level

    HIGH

    Safe Disposal Protocol

    • Do not throw in regular dustbins.
    • Do not flush tablets into drains or toilets.
    • Segregate expired medicines separately.
    • Place in yellow biomedical waste container.
    • Dispose through authorized incineration facility.

    Example

    Expired paracetamol tablets, antibiotic strips, unused prescription medicines.

    Used Insulin Vials

    Material Composition

    Glass Waste (Medical Glass with Drug Residue)

    Waste Category & Bin

    Blue Bin

    Hospital Department

    Pharmacy / Endocrinology Department / Ward

    Environmental Impact

    Broken glass vials can cause injury, while residual insulin contamination may affect waste-processing systems.

    Risk Level

    MEDIUM

    Safe Disposal Protocol

    • Do not break manually.
    • Place directly into blue puncture-proof container.
    • Disinfect if visibly contaminated.

      Expired Cytotoxic / Chemotherapy Drugs

      Material Composition

      Hazardous Pharmaceutical Waste (DNA-Damaging Chemical Compounds)

      Waste Category & Bin

      Yellow Bin

      Hospital Department

      Oncology Department / Chemotherapy Unit / Pharmacy

      Environmental Impact

      Cytotoxic compounds can damage DNA in living organisms, contaminate water systems, and severely affect aquatic ecosystems.

      Risk Level

      CRITICAL

      Safe Disposal Protocol

      • Handle using double gloves and full PPE.
      • Store in leak-proof labeled cytotoxic waste container.
      • Do not mix with regular pharmaceutical waste.
      • Seal securely for transport.
      • Dispose by high-temperature incineration only.

      Example

      Cisplatin, Methotrexate, Cyclophosphamide, Doxorubicin.

      Used Urinary Catheter / Foley Catheter

      Material Composition

      Waste Category & Bin

      Red Bin

      Hospital Department

      Urology Department / ICU / General Ward

      Environmental Impact

      Improper disposal of used catheters can spread infectious body fluids and contribute to plastic pollution in landfills and water systems.
